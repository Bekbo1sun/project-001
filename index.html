Moldo Kylych

Moldo Kylych son of Shamykan (Shamyrkan) uulu

(1866-1917) is one of the first Kyrgyz poets. He was born in Buguchu, Kochkor valley. Klych's own father, Shamyrkan, is the eldest son of the famous hero Tor√∂keldi. When Kylych was five years old, his father Shamyrkan returned from the world. Like his father, he grows up to be a smart and smart boy from a young age. His work called "Zilzala" was published in Kazan in 1911 in the Arabic alphabet. This situation indicates that it was one of the first published works in the history of the Kyrgyz people.

His works: "Chuy bayan", "Kerme mountain", "Birds", "Kol kazal", "Kara Kochkor", "Boy and girl", "Mal kazal", "Jatakchilar", "Chuy birds".

His works are rich in ethical thoughts about human behavior, proverbs, and educational ideas. The work "Chuy bayan" and "Buudayik" expresses the philosophical idea that going against nature can end in tragedy for human beings. Moldo Kylych's works were politically discriminated against by the totalitarian ideology for a long time and were closed to the "black box". Scientist Tazabek Samanchin, who wrote a scientific work about his work in the 40s, was persecuted as a "Nationalist" and "Cosmopolitan". The literary heritage of Moldo Kylych returned to the people during the years of democratic renewal, we do not know the name of his mother.
